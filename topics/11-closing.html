If you get your first prompt right

Lessons to carry away

  • You don't fill every lever. You choose.
    A throwaway question takes one line. A real design takes several. The judgment is knowing which.
  • Specificity and depth are dials, not switches.
    If you don't set them, the AI sets them for you, and it guesses average.
  • A clean restart beats ten corrections.
    When it's locked onto the wrong idea, start over with a better prompt instead of arguing it back.
  • Good prompting is just clear thinking, written down.
    If you can't say what you want, the AI can't either.
