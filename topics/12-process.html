Process

Process is the actual steps you want the agent to follow to get there. When you know the method that produces a good answer, handing it over is often the difference between a right answer and a confident wrong one.

Why it matters

Most prompts describe a destination and leave the route entirely to the agent. Sometimes that's fine. But for tasks where the way you reach the answer determines whether it's correct, leaving the route open is how you get answers that look right and aren't.

The trip makes this concrete. Say you ask for a standout dinner spot for a birthday on your last night in Kyoto. With no process, the agent pulls from whatever it already holds, which skews toward famous places and old information. Give it the route instead: check recent Google Maps reviews, cross-reference what people are recommending on Reddit this year, filter for places taking reservations on your date, then shortlist three with a line on why each fits a birthday. The answer stops being a guess from memory and starts being the result of a method you'd trust.

That's the lever. When you know the steps that lead to a good answer, write them into the prompt rather than hoping the agent picks the same path. Where Grounding hands over facts and Rules set the guardrails, Process hands over the method.

One thing worth saying on the slide: you don't always want to specify the process. If the agent's own approach is likely better than yours, leaving the route open is the smarter call, the same way discovery can beat direction. Process is a lever you reach for when the method matters to whether the answer is right, not a step you bolt onto every prompt.

Trip to Japan

Role

Travel agent who knows Japan

Task

Plan a trip to Japan.

Context

<Who's going.>

<When.>

Output Format

A day-by-day itinerary

Directions

Three days Tokyo, four in Kyoto, max $200 a night, day four open for a Nikko trip, no museums, hotels near a JR line.

One evening is my partner's birthday, make it special.

Process

Look for recommendations on Reddit, check recent Google Maps reviews, cross-reference what people are recommending on Reddit this year, then shortlist three with a line on why each fits a birthday.
