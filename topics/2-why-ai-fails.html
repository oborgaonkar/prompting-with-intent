Why AI Fails Predictably?

AI does not fail randomly. When you leave a gap in your prompt, it fills that gap with the most statistically average answer from its training. The failures are predictable once you see them as missing context, not missing intelligence.

Think about ordering coffee. If you walk up and say "give me a coffee," that's a completely valid request. The barista fills in every gap with whatever sells best. Size, milk, temperature, sweetness, all decided for you, based on what's popular, not what you actually wanted.

Now say "iced oat latte, two shots, light ice, vanilla, venti." Every gap you close gets you a more precise coffee. Oat instead of whole. Two shots instead of one. Light ice instead of standard. None of that is being difficult. It's just deciding what matters to you instead of letting someone else decide for you.

Same thing happens with AI. The fewer gaps you leave, the less it has to guess. And it doesn't guess randomly. It defaults to whatever's most common, the "whole milk" of its training data.
